# Lens 2 — Fundamentals

## Core question

Do the reported numbers — on a GAAP basis — support the story the stock is
telling?

## What to produce in this section

1. **Growth & quality.** Revenue growth (YoY and sequential), gross margin
   trend, operating margin, FCF. Always latest reported quarter + full-year
   context, with the period labeled.
2. **The GAAP-margin check.** Compare GAAP vs. non-GAAP margins. Large gaps
   driven by SBC, "one-time" items that recur, or interest income dressed as
   operating strength are red flags. If profit is mostly interest income or
   buybacks are SBC-funded while true FCF is negative, say so plainly.
3. **Balance sheet & dilution.** Cash runway, debt and interest burden,
   share count trend. An active large ATM program + heavy SBC is a
   near-disqualifier for small caps; small, strategic, or debt-retiring
   dilution can be acceptable — distinguish which one this is.
4. **Forward vs. trailing.** For pre-volume-ramp names (design wins,
   qualification stage), TTM multiples mislead; anchor on signed-contract
   forward revenue vs. market cap instead, and state the assumption chain.
   For mature names, use trailing multiples vs. history and peers.
5. **Valuation cross-check.** One primary multiple appropriate to the
   business model (EV/S for pre-profit growth, P/E or FCF yield for mature),
   compared against 2–3 named peers.

## Output

A compact metrics table (period-labeled), the GAAP-vs-non-GAAP verdict, the
dilution verdict, and one sentence on whether valuation is pricing the bull
case, base case, or wreck case.
