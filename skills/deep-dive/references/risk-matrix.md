# Lens 6 — Risk Matrix & Conviction

## Core question

Integrate the previous five lenses into a graded risk map and a conviction
tier with sizing logic. This section is written LAST and must reference the
other sections' findings — it is a synthesis, not a checklist rerun.

## Part A — Risk Matrix

Build a table of the 5–8 most material risks. For each:

| Risk | Likelihood (L/M/H) | Impact (L/M/H) | Transmission | Mitigant / monitor |

Standard categories to consider (include only the material ones):
- **Binary/structural:** single-customer concentration, China export ban,
  qualification failure, going-concern/refinancing, restructuring.
- **Dilution:** active ATM size vs. market cap, SBC run-rate, convertible
  overhang. A large live ATM on a small cap is a near-disqualifier — if
  present, cap the tier at C unless offset by signed contracts.
- **Competitive:** second-source qualification timeline, technology
  substitution, pricing-power erosion.
- **Execution:** ramp risk, capacity buildout, management credibility.
- **Macro/policy:** from Lens 3 — only the ones with concrete transmission.
- **Crowding/positioning:** from Lens 5 — gap risk if the trade is consensus.

## Part B — Conviction tier

Assign S/A/B/C/D/F:

- **S** — Fundamentally de-risked: creditworthy (Mag7-grade) counterparty
  + signed take-or-pay contracts + clean balance sheet + edge intact.
- **A** — Strong thesis, one open question (e.g. ramp execution).
- **B** — Credible but early or partially priced; needs a dated catalyst.
- **C** — Interesting process example; binary risks or dilution overhang
  dominate. Watchlist, not position.
- **D** — Story stock: narrative without GAAP support or chain evidence.
- **F** — Anti-pattern territory: financial-engineering valuation, layer
  confusion, or already-frontrun event chase.

## Part C — Sizing logic (qualitative only)

Size to conviction AND binariness, never conviction alone. High-conviction
binary microcaps: small size or defined-risk options; never "safer" naked
exposure just because conviction is high. Note holding-period fit (LTCG if
catalysts ≥12 months out) and whether margin/leverage is inappropriate in
the current macro regime. Never give specific position sizes, dollar
amounts, or order instructions.

## Output

The risk table, the tier with a one-paragraph justification citing at least
three of the previous five sections, upgrade/downgrade triggers, and the
sizing paragraph.
