# Lens 5 — Sentiment & Positioning

## Core question

Who already knows this story, and is the crowd's read an edge or a trap?
House view: retail sentiment is usually a contrarian or null signal —
"IGNORE the sentiment since it's usually wrong." What matters is the GAP
between evidence and positioning.

## What to produce in this section

1. **Coverage & institutional lag.** How many analysts cover it, recent
   rating/target changes, institutional ownership trend. The best setups
   show supply-chain/fundamental evidence RUNNING AHEAD of coverage —
   validation lag is the edge. Saturated bullish coverage means the edge
   is gone.
2. **Retail temperature.** X/Reddit/StockTwits presence: is it undiscovered,
   discussed by a few specialists, or a meme? Meme status raises volatility
   and gap risk regardless of thesis quality.
3. **Narrative stage.** Classify: (a) undiscovered, (b) specialist-known,
   (c) narrative forming, (d) consensus, (e) euphoria/crowded. Entries
   favor a–c; d–e require the fundamentals to keep beating.
4. **Noise filters.** Insider sales are noise, not a bear signal. Confident
   media commentary without domain fluency is noise. Weight sources by
   demonstrated supply-chain/financial specificity, not follower count or
   credentials.
5. **What would flip sentiment.** The one headline or datapoint that would
   move the crowd from current stage to the next — that's usually the
   catalyst the Technicals section should date.

## Output

Narrative-stage classification with 2–3 pieces of evidence, the
institutional-lag verdict (edge intact / partially priced / crowded), and
one line on how sentiment risk affects sizing.
